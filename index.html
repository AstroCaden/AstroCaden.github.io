About Me

I am an undergraduate astrophysics student with interests in compact objects, gravitational-wave modelling, and computational astrophysics. My recent work at IFJ PAN in Kraków focused on studying the structure of hybrid neutron stars within the Einstein Telescope detection range. I implemented and refined a Tolman–Oppenheimer–Volkoff (TOV) solver, converting it from C++ to Python to analyse over 100 candidate equations of state, investigating their mass–radius relations, pressure–density profiles, and tidal deformabilities.

I am passionate about using numerical modelling and data analysis to understand the physics of extreme astrophysical environments.
