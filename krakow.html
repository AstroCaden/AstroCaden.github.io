Overview

This study models the structure of neutron stars with possible deconfined quark cores (hybrid stars) and summarises their gravitational-wave–relevant properties for the Einstein Telescope context. A Python implementation of a TOV solver (converted from C++) was used to generate mass–radius and tidal-deformability sequences for a set of hybrid equations of state (EoS). Results are compared with constraints consistent with GW170817.

Hybrid Equations of State & Phase Transition

Hybrid EoS were constructed by joining hadronic and quark phases via a first-order transition. In the pressure–μ view, the onset and relative stiffness of the quark sector are visible as distinct segments or kinks.
