Overview

This study models the structure of neutron stars with possible deconfined quark cores (hybrid stars) and summarises their gravitational-wave–relevant properties for the Einstein Telescope context. A Python implementation of a TOV solver (converted from C++) was used to generate mass–radius and tidal-deformability sequences for a set of hybrid equations of state (EoS). Results are compared with constraints consistent with GW170817. The descriptions below follow the figures and wording used in the project report and presentation.

Hybrid Equations of State & Phase Transition

Hybrid EoS were constructed by joining hadronic and quark phases via a first-order transition. In the pressure–μ view, the onset and relative stiffness of the quark sector are visible as distinct segments or kinks, matching the presentation’s overview of how parameter choices shape the transition behaviour. The figure emphasises the part of the parameter space used to feed the stellar-structure calculation rather than specific microphysical derivations.

In pressure–ε, the transition appears as a change in slope between branches. As summarised in the report, stronger discontinuities map to unstable regions in the sequence of stellar models, which is the precondition for a separated, re-stabilised hybrid branch (“twin-star” behaviour). The panel is used as the EoS input diagnostic for subsequent TOV modelling rather than for extracting absolute thermodynamic parameters here.

TOV Modelling & Mass–Radius Sequences

The Python solver integrates the TOV equations for each EoS to generate full M–R sequences. The example shown reproduces the expected pattern from the presentation: a stable branch, an intermediate unstable region associated with the transition, and a re-stabilised hybrid branch at smaller radii. The emphasis is on the qualitative pattern (presence of the branch and the separation) rather than absolute values here.

Tidal Deformability

For gravitational-wave applications, the presentation highlights Λ(M) as the diagnostic linking compactness to waveform phasing. The example panel shows the steep fall of Λ with increasing mass and the behaviour associated with entering the hybrid-core regime. Trends are discussed qualitatively in line with the report text rather than used for numerical inference on this page.

Summary

The figures above, drawn from the report and presentation, summarise the workflow: hybrid EoS construction with a first-order transition, TOV-based M–R generation including hybrid branches, and tidal-deformability diagnostics used for gravitational-wave comparisons. The qualitative behaviours match the presented results: the emergence of a re-stabilised hybrid branch under a sufficiently strong transition and the associated decrease in Λ for more compact configurations.
